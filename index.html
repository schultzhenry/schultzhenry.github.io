116th Zine (2018)

In partial fulfillment of Yale's Graphic Design Methodologies course with Pamela Hovland, I designed this zine to celebrate the historic representation of women in the 116th Congress.

The body of the zine lists all congressional representatives from the 1st Congress, which began meeting in 1789, to the 116th Congress, elected in 2018. Names of women are printed in green.

10 copies of the zine were printed for the Odds and Ends Art Book Fair.

Engender (2016-2019)

In 2016, events involving race and gender-based discrimination motivated a group of classmates and I to correct structural inequalities on our campus. To organize around this goal, we founded Engender.

Engender advocates for reforming Greek organizations given their discriminatory nature and disproportionate control over undergraduate social life. The existing structure of these organizations enables harmful behaviors including hazing, substance abuse, sexual misconduct, and acts of hate that occur frequently on American college campuses to the detriment of students' mental and physical wellbeing.

My personal responsibilities for Engender have included communications, identity, branding, member onboarding, and organizing a group of women and nonbinary students to rush a fraternity.

See Engender's website, Facebook or Twitter.
